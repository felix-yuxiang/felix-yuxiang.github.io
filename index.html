About Me

I'm an undergraduate student pursuing an Honours Bsc. degree at the UBC. My current summer work involves developing bioinformatic pipelines to process high dimensional sequencing data, using Probabilistic Programming (PyroPyMC) for Bayesian modeling to infer clone population based on genomic alteration profiles extracted from ctDNA. MCMC and VI are used to address the intractable integrals common in Bayesian inference. PPL makes the implementation of graphical model efficient. A side project is quality control for imaging mass cytometry (IMC) data.

My favorite sport is basketball. I love to cook, go hiking, watch movies/series/NBA games in my spare time.
